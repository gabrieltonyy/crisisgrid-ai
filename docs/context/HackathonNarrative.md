# 🚨 The Core Problem

In many parts of the world—especially in developing regions like Kenya:

- Emergencies are detected first by **citizens**
- But systems are designed for **top-down reporting**
- Information is:
  - Fragmented
  - Delayed
  - Unverified
  - Hard to act on

This leads to:

- Preventable accidents
- Delayed emergency response
- Public panic
- Poor coordination between agencies

# 💡 The Idea

What if every citizen could become a **real-time crisis sensor**?

What if AI could:

- Verify what they see
- Understand the situation
- Alert the right people
- Guide others to safety

In seconds.

# 🌍 Introducing CrisisGrid AI

CrisisGrid AI is a **real-time crisis intelligence platform** that transforms:

> Scattered citizen observations → Verified, actionable emergency intelligence

---

# 🧠 How It Works (Simple Story)

### Step 1 — A Citizen Sees Danger

A user spots:

- A fire in a building  
- Flooding on a road  
- A wild animal in a residential area  

They open CrisisGrid AI and:

- Take a photo  
- Share location  
- Submit report  

---

### Step 2 — AI Verifies the Situation

Behind the scenes:

- Verification Agent checks the image  
- GeoRisk Agent determines affected area  
- Context Agents (weather/wildlife) add intelligence  

The system answers:

> Is this real?  
> How serious is it?  
> Who is affected?  

---

### Step 3 — The System Takes Action

Within seconds:

- Nearby users are alerted  
- Authorities are notified (simulated in MVP)  
- Safety guidance is generated  

---

### Step 4 — People Stay Safe

Users receive simple instructions:

- “Avoid this road”  
- “Move to higher ground”  
- “Stay indoors”
